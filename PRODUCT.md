# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Clients who log in to manage their own ad campaigns, products, and creative styles. They are not necessarily technical, but they are results-oriented: they want to create, launch, and monitor ad campaigns with minimal friction. They work during business hours on desktop, likely in a focused work session rather than a casual browse.

## Product Purpose

An ad campaign management dashboard that lets clients orchestrate the full lifecycle of advertising: define products, set styles, generate AI-powered ad creatives, and run campaigns. Success means a client can go from brief to live campaign with confidence, without needing to open another tool.

## Brand Personality

Professional, precise, quietly confident. The tool should feel like a trusted partner for serious marketing work, not a toy or a bloated enterprise system. Three words: **sharp, composed, capable**.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS (HubSpot-style): blue gradients, rounded-everything, stock-photo visual language
- Meta Ads Manager: cluttered, overwhelming density, dark-pattern navigation
- Google Ads UI: joylessly utilitarian, no sense of craft
- AI-generated slop: purple gradient heroes, glassmorphism cards, hero-metric layouts, identical card grids

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity earns trust.** Clients are spending money. Every number, status, and action must be unambiguous at a glance.
2. **Hierarchy over decoration.** Visual weight communicates importance. Never decorate where you could clarify.
3. **The work is the product.** Campaign creatives and results are the star. The chrome should recede.
4. **Confident defaults.** Prefer sensible defaults over open-ended forms. Reduce decision fatigue.
5. **No surprises.** Destructive or expensive actions get confirmation. Status is always visible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Ensure sufficient color contrast for text and interactive elements in both light and dark themes. Support keyboard navigation throughout. Reduced-motion preference should disable or soften transitions.
